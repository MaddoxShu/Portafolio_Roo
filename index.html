  • Agost 29,2021 Videogame Day "It's not just entertainment"

    Dostie Memorie

    Human Connections Media together with the rest of the world celebrates every August 29 since 2008, Video Game Day or Gamer's Day, an idea that emerged from the main magazines in the market and ended up consolidating. In the past gamers were considered a bit "geeks" but today it is rare to find a home where there is no Playstation, Nintendo or XBOX console.

    Gaming is already part of a culture and more and more fans are putting aside other forms of entertainment to grow the universe of millions of gamers in the world. According to Human Connections Media research, this market has grown so much in Mexico that there are already more than 70 million gamers (Competitive Intelligence Unit). This is reflected in searches and purchases in Mercado Libre where the category represents 6% of the total income of this e-commerce giant and gamers spend an average of MX $ 2,500. The four best-selling products are gaming chairs, video cards, consoles, and monitors.

    Mexico is the number one country in number of gamers and in market value, reaching the figure of 30,000 million pesos in Latin America according to a Verizon Media report consulted by Human Connections Media. Additionally, it has the highest penetration of gamers on any device (92%) and is the country where the highest percentage of users own a game console (55%). The same was found in this regard regarding the type of gamers: Casual / mobile, enjoy games without investing too much time (74% are women). On consoles, you pay a premium price for dedicated hardware and play games on your TV, as well as streaming and entertainment (66% of these are households with children). The hardcore / PC gamer, invests a lot of time and money in video games, (57% are men). The streamer / competitor: shares their gameplay and interacts with the community (85% belong to Generation Z or Millennial).

    Latin America is the market with the highest growth in the world, reaching US $ 6 billion in revenue in 2020, positioning itself as one of the few growing sectors during the economic crisis that affected the region. However, the Latin American video game sector still has many limitations that prevent it from fully exploiting its growth potential. One of the reasons underlying this situation is the lack of studies and specific knowledge about this sector, which limits the ability of public policy makers to design and implement effective interventions.

  • September 07,2021 Eight key disciplines to become a video game develope

    Believe in Yourself

    If you are a gamer at heart and have considered developing video games as a career option, but do not know the disciplines that every aspirant needs to master, then Juan Gabriel Gomila, mathematician and data scientist at Udemy Business, shares the knowledge to acquire to become in one. Proof that this career will be more in demand in the future, according to data from the consultancy The Competitive Intelligence Unit, “more than 90% of those under 20 years of age, at least two-thirds of the population between 21 and 40 years old are gamers, while a third of adults over 50 years of age at least play on cell phones ”(1). In general terms, the work of a video game developer includes its conception, design, development and commercialization, as well as the coordination of interdisciplinary teams in charge of each of these stages. It is important to highlight that a video game is possible thanks to the participation of different specialized professionals, so knowing the basic principles of the following disciplines will be important to have a comprehensive vision as a developer:

    Narrative (storytelling): it is one of the most important parts since it is the central story of the video game, it helps to determine the role that each character will have, their physical and psychological characteristics, their powers, challenges and obstacles that they have to face, as well as the contexts in which they will be developed and the multiple scenarios in which they will be involved depending on the plot.

    Basic principles of psychology: both of the characters and of the users. Regarding the former, it gives them personality, defines their psychological profiles and with it their behavior, abilities, powers and weaknesses; On the players' side, it defines the incentives throughout the different stages of the video game, the rewards they will access and the way they interact with each of the characters in a way that maintains their interest and motivation to continue playing.

    Mathematics: its application is to determine the distribution and creation of models of probabilities of outcomes or continuity according to different actions in each of the levels, as well as to balance the difficulty throughout the game. The objective of this is to avoid the fatigue or frustration of the player.

    Physics: having to simulate real objects in a virtual world, it becomes necessary to know basic physics (rotation, gravity, friction, etc.). to recreate their behavior, almost exactly, in the face of different actions to give the game realism. For example, if you are going to recreate a river, you will need to know fluid mechanics to reproduce it as well as possible.

    Marketing: to monetize the video game it will be necessary to go on the market to promote and sell it, for which to know costs, the target audience, communication channels, marketing strategies, campaign generation, key metrics to measure its impact, conversion ratios, among others, they will be of vital importance. It will be of little use to have the best video game if people do not know it and above all, do not buy it.

    Programming: it is a basic one since you will have to develop a lot of code with steps to follow for each of the actions of the characters according to the instructions of the player and indirect phenomena of these.

    Art: it is the aesthetic part of the video game, here it is necessary to know basic principles of music, lighting, kinematics, modeling, texturing and 3D animation; These aspects are of the utmost importance since it is the most visible and tangible part of the virtual world in which the players operate.

    Economics: using game theory helps to determine how fair a rule is and establishes criteria to balance the criteria to move from one level to another according to certain gains, for example virtual currencies.
